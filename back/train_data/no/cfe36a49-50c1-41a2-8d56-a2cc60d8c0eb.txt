berlin ( reuters ) - spanish prime minister mariano rajoy said in a newspaper interview that spain would not be divided , a week after catalonia held a referendum that authorities there say showed voters overwhelmingly support independence .
asked if there was a risk that spain would be divided , rajoy said in an interview published in die welt on monday : absolutely not .
spain will not be divided and national unity will be preserved .
we ll do everything that legislation allows to ensure that .
catalonia , which has its own language and culture and is led by a pro-independence regional government , held the oct. 1 referendum over secession in defiance of spain s constitutional court which had declared the vote illegal .
