london ( reuters ) - significant gaps still remain between parties attempting to restore northern ireland s power-sharing administration , british prime minister theresa may s office said on monday after she spoke with irish counterpart leo varadkar .
may and varadkar agreed in a phonecall that it was in the interests of everyone that the executive was restored , but differences remained over issues such as the irish language .
northern ireland has been without a devolved administration since its collapse in january , raising the prospect of direct rule being re-imposed from london .
